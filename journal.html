Retreat at HCDC Maritime Training Center

I had the incredible opportunity to attend a retreat at the HCDC Maritime Training Center located in Camudmud, Samal Island. This experience helped me not only in improving my technical skills but also in developing leadership qualities through various workshops and activities.

During the retreat, we engaged in several team-building exercises, reflected on ways to care for Mother Earth, and participated in activities that emphasized sustainability and environmental care. It was a perfect mix of both personal growth and environmental responsibility.

Spiritual Reflection

The retreat was not only a time for learning new skills but also a time to reflect spiritually. The theme of the retreat, "Pilgrims of Hope: Walking lightly, Caring deeply", encouraged us to embrace a journey of purpose and compassion.

Two Bible verses particularly resonated with me during this time:

John 14:6 (NIV): "Jesus answered, 'I am the way and the truth and the life. No one comes to the Father except through me.'

Jeremiah 29:11 (NIV): "For I know the plans I have for you," declares the Lord, "plans to prosper you and not to harm you, plans to give you a hope and a future."

These verses reminded me that our path in life is guided by faith, and that with purpose and hope, we can face any challenge that comes our way. As pilgrims, we are called to walk lightly, unburdened by the weight of the world, yet deeply invested in caring for others and fulfilling our God-given purpose.

Through "Micro Actions," such as reducing waste, conserving energy, and protecting nature, we can have a positive impact on our environment. Together, these actions lead to "Macro Contributions," where collective care for the Earth can result in meaningful change.
